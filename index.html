    • Gulab Jamum

      When we think of Indian food, our minds are likely to travel to rich, fragrant curries and crisp snacks like pakoras or samosas. Yet India has a food culture rich with super sweet desserts. Gulab jamun is one of the most popular Indian sweets appearing at festivals, birthdays and weddings. While soft little dumplings soaked in syrup exist around the world, what sets gulab jamun apart is that the dumplings are made from milk. Traditionally, milk was simmered until only the solids remained, which were then mixed with maida flour to form little balls.
